What is Alzheimer’s Disease?

Alzheimer’s is a progerssive brain disorder that over time destroys memory and cognitive function. It occurs as cells in the brain continuously degenerate and die. As of 2014, Alzheimer’s has afflicted over 5 million people in the US. The disease is less common amongst younger individuals, and the risk increases with age, notably past the age of 60 years.

What are common symptoms? See for yourself.

Alzheimer’s can impact diseased individuals cognitively, behaviorally, and psychologically. The main symptoms of Alzheimer’s are memory loss and confusion. Other symptoms include the following: Confusion, forgetfulness, disorientation, and difficulty understanding concepts

Behavioral: Getting lost, irritability, restlessness

Psychological: Paranoia, hallucination

Risk Factors:

Age: The primary risk factor of Alzheimer’s is increased age. As you get older, the chance of of developing the condition increases. Other risk factors include, family history, past head trauma, poor lifestyle and poor heart health
